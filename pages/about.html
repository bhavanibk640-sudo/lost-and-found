Design Thinking & Idea Lab Project

About This Project

The Ramaiah Lost & Found Portal is a student-built web application developed as part of the Design Thinking & Idea Lab course at MS Ramaiah College of Engineering, Bengaluru.

The project addresses a real, everyday problem faced by students on campus — the absence of a centralized, digital system to report and recover lost belongings. Before this app, students relied on WhatsApp groups and physical notice boards, which are inefficient, limited in reach, and easy to miss.

Our Process

Design Thinking Framework

1

Empathize

We interviewed 10 students across different departments at Ramaiah. 8 out of 10 had lost something on campus in the past year. Zero of them had a reliable way to report or recover it beyond posting in WhatsApp groups. Students reported frustration, wasted time, and resigned acceptance that lost items would never be returned.

2

Define

Problem Statement: "Ramaiah students have no centralized, accessible system to report lost items or claim found ones — leading to unnecessary loss of belongings and reduced campus trust."

3

Ideate

We brainstormed three solutions: (a) a WhatsApp bot that collects reports via messages, (b) a physical notice board QR code system, and (c) a web application with search and photo upload. We evaluated each on reach, ease of use, and zero-cost feasibility. The web app won on all three.

4

Prototype

We built a working web application using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript for the frontend, with Firebase (free tier) as the database and image storage backend. The app was hosted free on GitHub Pages and tested with real students within 2 weeks of starting development.

5

Test

We ran a one-week test with 5 classmates. During this period, 3 items were posted and 2 were successfully returned to their owners — a 67% resolution rate in the first week alone. Students gave feedback that the WhatsApp contact feature was the most useful part of the app.

Project Impact

Why this matters for Ramaiah

MS Ramaiah College has over 10,000 students spread across multiple blocks, hostels, and facilities. Items get lost every single day — ID cards, phones, keys, laptops. Without a system, most of these are never returned. This portal gives every student a free, instant, campus-specific tool that works on any device. It costs nothing to run, requires no installation, and connects students directly via WhatsApp — the communication tool they already use.
